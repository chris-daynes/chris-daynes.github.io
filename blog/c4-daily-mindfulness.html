4.4 Cultural Blog

What did you learn from your 'mindfulness conversation'? How did the other person find it?

I choose to do the informal mindfulness conversation. I gave the 'gift' of my full attention to my family when they were talking to me. I am guilty of listening with my mind churning over other things. I am unsure how they found it but I found it really made me make the choice to give them my full attention. So I certainly felt better for it. It removed the guilt I feel when I often do the opposite!

Which of the three suggested techniques will you use to sustain your practice?.

The saying by Mingyur resonated with me "Although I liked the idea of meditation, I didn't like the practice of meditation". It allowed me to acknowledge that instigating mediation practice can be hard. Even for the experts! From the author, the process of taking even just one mindfull breath per day was particularly encouraging.

Any other musings?

I have been doing at least one mediatition per day. This has been really beneficial. Often difficult though being on holiday in tight accommodation. When I'm back in NZ I will gift myself twice per day sessions.
